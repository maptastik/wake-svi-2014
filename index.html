This vizualization examines the Center for Disease Control's (CDC) Social Vulnerability Index (SVI) 2014 values for Wake County, North Carolina. A compilation of several demographic indicators, the SVI serves as a useful tool for identifying areas that are likely to require the greatest need in times of crisis.

15 social factors are divided amongst 4 major themes. Factor scores are tabulated across the 4 themes and combined to create a final SVI value for each US Census Tract. Those themes area:

  • Socioeconomic
    • Below Poverty
    • Unemployed
    • Income
    • No High School Diploma
  • Housing Composition and Disability
    • Aged 65 or Older
    • Aged 17 or Younger
    • Civilian with a Disability
    • Single-Parent Household
  • Minority Status and Language
    • Minority
    • Speak English "Less than Well"
  • Housing and Transportation
    • Multi-Unit Structures
    • Mobile Homes
    • Crowding
    • No Vehicle
    • Group Quarters

This visualation enables the user to examine the distribution of scores for each of these major themes as well as the overall SVI for each Census Tract in Wake County. The data used in this visualization were originally released by the CDC and accessed through Wake County, NC's Open Data Portal. More information on the data and methodology for calculating SVI are available from the CDC.
